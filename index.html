WIRE FAULT DETECTOR

Non-contact · Glide-over-insulation · Beep-at-fault

How It Behaves
PROBE SLIDING OVER WIRE →
🔦
⚠ BEEP — FAULT HERE ⚠
Working Principle
Transmitter side: Connect one end of the wire to a ~1kHz oscillator (555 timer). This injects an AC signal into the wire.
EM field radiates: The wire acts like an antenna and radiates a small electromagnetic field uniformly along its length.
Pickup coil detects: A small coil (10–20 turns on ferrite) in your probe picks up this field as you glide it over the wire.
At the fault: Break = signal stops. Short = signal distorts. Melt = field changes. The coil output drops/changes suddenly.
LM358 amplifies the coil signal and drives a buzzer. Sudden drop → buzzer changes tone or stops → you found the fault!

Build Steps
Make the transmitter: Wire NE555 in astable mode with 10kΩ and 47kΩ resistors + 10µF cap. This generates ~1kHz square wave signal.
Connect to wire: Attach the 555 output to one end of the wire under test. Other end left open (for break detection) or grounded (for short detection).
Wind pickup coil: Wind 20 turns of thin copper wire on a ferrite rod (or even an iron nail). This is your probe head that you glide over the wire.
Amplify with LM358: Connect coil output to + input of LM358. Add 100kΩ feedback resistor. The op-amp boosts the tiny coil signal.
Connect buzzer: Wire buzzer to LM358 output. As you slide coil over the wire, buzzer beeps continuously. At the fault point — tone changes or stops.
Test it: Deliberately break a test wire and verify the device detects it. Adjust sensitivity by changing the feedback resistor value.
⚠
Only use on de-energized (switched OFF) wires. Never test on live mains wiring. This circuit works with the 9V injected signal only — not with 220V AC.
